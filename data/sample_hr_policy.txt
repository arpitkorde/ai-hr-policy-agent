1. LEAVE POLICY
-----------------

1.1 Annual Leave
Employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
New employees joining mid-year will receive prorated leave based on their
start date. Unused leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 5 days
to the next calendar year. Any leave beyond 5 days will lapse.

1.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical
certificate is required for sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days.
Unused sick leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

1.3 Parental Leave
Maternity leave: 26 weeks of paid leave for the primary caregiver.
Paternity leave: 2 weeks of paid leave for the secondary caregiver.
Adoption leave: 12 weeks of paid leave for the primary adopting parent.

1.4 Work From Home
Employees may work from home up to 2 days per week with manager approval.
Remote work requests exceeding 2 days require VP-level approval.

2. CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------

2.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain professional conduct in the workplace.
Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind will not be tolerated
and may result in immediate termination.

2.2 Dress Code
Business casual attire is expected Monday through Thursday.
Casual attire is permitted on Fridays.

2.3 Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose proprietary company information to external
parties without written authorization from their department head.

3. COMPENSATION & BENEFITS
----------------------------

3.1 Salary Reviews
Performance reviews and salary adjustments occur annually in April.
Mid-year reviews may be conducted in October at the manager's discretion.

3.2 Health Insurance
The company provides comprehensive health insurance covering:
- Employee: 100% premium covered
- Spouse: 80% premium covered
- Children (up to 3): 80% premium covered
Maximum coverage: $500,000 per family per year.

3.3 Retirement Benefits
The company matches employee 401(k) contributions up to 6% of base salary.
Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years.

3.4 Learning & Development
Each employee has an annual learning budget of $2,000 for courses,
certifications, and conferences. Requests must be approved by the
reporting manager and HR.

4. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
------------------------

4.1 Reporting
Employees may report grievances to their manager, HR, or through the
anonymous ethics hotline at ethics@acmecorp.com.

4.2 Investigation
All grievances will be investigated within 10 business days.
The employee will be informed of the outcome within 5 business days
after investigation completion.

4.3 Retaliation
The company has a strict anti-retaliation policy. Any employee found
retaliating against a complainant will face disciplinary action.
